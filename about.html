About Me







Hello, my name is Luis Garcia. I am a Data Analyst/Developer based in Austin, TX. I currently work for a neuroscience research company, developing databases and reports that provide insights for the company. My background consists of an education in the field of psychology with a strong focus on research methods and more recently a certification in data analysis and visualization. I have worked in both academic and private settings conducting research, which has allowed me to learn valuable insights into how both these fields operate.
My passion has always revolved around creating, building and designing just about anything that I can get my hands on. Early on, I taugh myself how to draw and paint, which allowed me to channel all my energy into creating abstract art. Later in college I channeled that into designing studies, which lead me to my career in research. At last my passion for creating found the world of coding, and developing systems.




Questions


Where are you from

Origins


I am originally from Monterrey, Nuevo Leon in Mexico. I spent most of my life moving from city to city in Mexico eventually moving to the United States and later becoming a U.S. citizen. In the United States I have mostly lived in the great state of Texas and most recently decided to settle down for a while here in Austin, TX.
All in all, my expereince moving around all my life has taught me to value different cultures and embrace all sorts of people from varying backgrounds.

Where did you go to school

Education


I graduated from the Univerity of Texas at Austin with a B.A. in Psychology and a Minor in English. Through out my college career I focused mainly on the research aspect of psychology. I was mostly interested in the real world applications of psychology and how to adapt it to my professional and personal life. Lets go Longhorns!
A couple of years after graduating I entered a certificate program for data analysis and visualization. It was a 6 month intensive program focused on developing and polishing technical programming skills in Excel, Python, Javascript, SQL Databases, Tableau, Big Data and Machine Learning.

How did you get involved in school

Internship


I began getting involved in school on the first half of my sophmore year. I joined the Conceptual Knowledge Lab ran by Dr. Reeves at the University of Texas at Austin. The lab's main focus was on the effects of language on cognition (figurative language, etc.).
By my last year in the intership I was in charge of developing study materials using python, which would later be the center of the testing in the lab. I also got the oportunity to represent the school by presenting the project's findings in two different conferences.

What are your interests?

Interests


I enjoy learning new things related to programing, psychology and art. For programing my main interest is the development and visualization of databases. For psychology my main interest is in I/O psychology particularly decision making and imrpoving workplace efficiency. Lastly I enjoy creating abstract art and learning how other people see the world.
